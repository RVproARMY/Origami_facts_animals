Facts about camels

      Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
      Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
      Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Facts about teddy-bear

      The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
      The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
      They aren’t just for children – teddy are for adults, and a quarter of us still has our childhood companion.

Facts about Pandas

      They spend a lot of their day eating.
      They have great camouflage for their environment.
      Bamboo is critical to their diet.

Facts about cicada

      The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
      Most have red-orange eyes.
      Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs.
